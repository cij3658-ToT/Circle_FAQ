Questions worth asking

Before you join us

What most leaders want to know before stepping in. If something isn't here, write to us — we'd rather have the real conversation.

How is this different from the hundreds of other leadership communities out there? +

Most leadership communities are networks that perform activity — events, content libraries, threads. Ours is built around one thing: dialogue between a small group of leaders who don't have to pretend. The Circle is eight people, same time every week, twelve weeks, no rotation. That's not a feature list — that's the whole offer.

If what you need is more content or more connections, we're not the right place. If what you need is a small room where you can say what is actually true about where you are — we might be.

What if I can't make a Circle session live? +

Circle sessions are not recorded. The room only works if everyone in it has agreed that what is said stays in the room — and that's incompatible with a recording sitting on a server afterwards. We ask Circle members to treat the weekly 90 minutes as a standing, protected commitment for twelve weeks.

If you miss a session, your cohort holds the thread for you, and you pick it up the following week. The Master Classes in the Threshold, by contrast, are recorded — so if the live calendar is genuinely impossible right now, the Threshold is a better place to begin.

Can I bring a real challenge I'm facing in my team? +

Yes — this is most of what happens in the room. The weekly dialogue begins with a prompt drawn from regenerative leadership practice, and then goes where the cohort needs it to go. That often means one member placing something real on the table — a decision they're avoiding, a relationship that has gone quiet, a moment they didn't handle well — and the seven others thinking alongside them. Names, companies, and specifics stay confidential.

Is this a training programme? +

No. Training transfers knowledge from someone who has it to someone who doesn't. That's not what is needed here. The leaders we work with have enough knowledge — what they're short of is a place to think honestly, with people who can challenge them without agenda.

You'll leave the Circle with frameworks and practices you can use. But the real outcome is that you'll have thought more clearly for twelve weeks than you usually get to, in the company of seven others doing the same.

So I pay and I'm in? +

The Threshold is free — you join by taking the Scorecard.

The Circle is different. It's by application, because the cohort only works when the eight people in it are right for each other. You write short answers to three questions, have a twenty-minute conversation with Elly or Chris, and we decide together whether this Circle is the right room for you. If it isn't — for you or for the cohort — we'll say so honestly and point you back to the Threshold. The Sanctuary is by invitation only.

When does the next Circle start? +

Two cohorts a year — late spring and early autumn. Threshold members hear about each intake a week before it opens publicly. If you're not yet a Threshold member, taking the Scorecard is the best way to stay close to what's next.

What's the commitment — and can I cancel? +

The Circle runs for twelve weeks. If you pay monthly, there's a three-month minimum — the cohort doesn't work if people leave mid-way through, so the commitment protects the other seven as much as it protects you.

Full refund if you withdraw before the second session. After that, no refunds — again, because the seven people who stay deserve a room that holds. If the Circle genuinely isn't right for you after twelve weeks, you simply don't re-enrol; if it is, there's the option to continue with a rolling cohort.

What happens after the twelve weeks? +

You keep access to the Threshold for six months — the Monday Prompt, the Master Classes, the Practice Library — as a bridge, at no extra cost.

Many Circle members stay in touch with the two or three people from their cohort who became real thinking partners. That's not something we engineer — it tends to happen on its own. Some choose to re-enrol in a continuing Circle cohort. And a small number of members, over time, are invited into the Sanctuary.

I'm not sure where I fit. What should I do? +

Start with the Scorecard. Fifteen questions, seven minutes. You'll get back a reading of where you are across four dimensions of regenerative leadership, along with three practices to begin with — and a clearer sense of which tier meets you where you are. No email wall, nothing to unsubscribe from.
